01 / What We Are Solving

The system you have built works. It just does not scale by hand.

You walk markets, identify the right five buildings on every drive, write 40 to 50 offers a year personally, run 80 across the team, and you watch most of those buildings sit because there is no system that keeps working on them while you go to the next market.

You also walk into ICSC waiting for retailers to hand you void maps. By then the corridors you would have proactively pitched are already on someone else's list. The instincts are already correct. The question is what carries them across three hundred zones, ten states, and the years it takes for a Pizza Hut to become a Chipotle.

"What I have learned from doing this two hundred times is when I drive a market, my favorite five sites are the ones I want to go buy. I come back in three or four years and probably all five have turned into Chipotle, Panera, Whataburger, Chick-fil-A, Valvoline. My instincts of what five sites to chase were correct. The problem is we get busy and go on to the next market, and there is not a system to keep working on those five buildings."

Darren Pitts, April 14

That is the gap. The plan below is how we close it: not one agent, but a chain of agents organized into a small number of architectural layers, built one at a time inside the boot camp, paid for by the Humans+Agents platform engagement, and run by your team on tools you already know.

02 / How We Build It Together

QAIZR coaches. The Humans+Agents platform hosts. Your team runs it.

Velocity Retail Group goes onto the Humans+Agents platform alongside QAIZR coaching. The boot camp is a month long. The first five days are the Sprint Week, where we build Agent Number One together end to end, against a real example you provide. Days Six through Thirty extend the chain, integrate the agent into your daily workflow, and train at least one teammate to operate it independently.

The work is people-powered and AI-accelerated. The agents take the toil. Your judgment, your instincts, and your relationships still drive every deal. The system simply lets you do across three hundred zones what today you can only do one market at a time.

03 / The Architectural Shape

Three layers. Six agents. Stacked so each one unlocks the next.

The system you described is not a single workflow. It is three workflows running on different clocks, and each wants a different shape.

Layer One

Active-Deal Engine

Hub-and-spoke. When a retailer hires you, an orchestrator dispatches specialists down the ladder: market plan, prospect list, owner research, offers. Runs per assignment.

Layer Two

Persistent-Monitoring Swarm

Always-on. Watches three hundred zones across ten sun-belt states for property-listed events and follow-up windows. Feeds new prospects into the deal engine. Never sleeps.

Layer Three

Proactive-Pitch Engine

On-demand void analysis. Given a market, identifies the retailers who should be there but are not. The mechanism behind your secret advantage at ICSC.

04 / The Six Candidate Agents

Each one is bounded, testable, and earns its place in the chain.

Six agents cover the workflow you described, with the Sprint pick called out first. Each card lists what the agent eats, what it produces, and the outcome you feel.

Agent 01 Sprint Pick

Owner Research Agent

Eats
A list of property addresses inside a defined zone (the fourteen prospects, listed plus off-market).
Produces
A clean record per address: entity name, owner name, current email, current phone, source confidence.

Replaces the source in India. Day Six, you wake up to fourteen verified contact records ready for outreach.

Agent 02

Offer-Writing Agent

Eats
Approved deal terms, pricing parameters, the property owner record, the buy criteria for the client.
Produces
A three-page non-binding letter of intent on your pre-approved form, ready for review.

Compresses the 80 offers a year. Brent and Victor stop typing. You review, sign, send.

Agent 03

Market Plan Generator

Eats
The retailer's void map, the market boundaries, the corridor data, the buy criteria.
Produces
A tiered market plan (tier one, tier two, tier three) with retail-gravity scoring and corridor recommendations.

Turns a vague client void map into a market plan in hours instead of weeks.

Agent 04

Corridor Monitor (Swarm)

Eats
Your three hundred defined zones, plus listings feeds (CoStar, Crexi, LoopNet).
Produces
Real-time alerts when a property hits the market in any zone you care about, ranked by fit.

Turns Layer Two on. You stop driving randomly. The system tells you where to look.

Agent 05

Long-Cadence Outreach

Eats
The off-market owner records, your preferred touch cadence, your voice and tone.
Produces
Personalized seller touches every quarter or six months, plus the responses surfaced for your judgment.

Ends the one-night-stand pattern. The robot sits on those five buildings for years so you don't have to.

Agent 06

Void Analysis (Proactive)

Eats
A market, demographics, retailer presence, peer-market patterns, retailer site criteria.
Produces
A pitch-ready packet: which retailers should be in this market, why, plus the sites you have in hand.

The secret advantage. You walk into ICSC armed instead of waiting on someone else's void map.

05 / The Sprint Week (Days One through Five)

We build Owner Research together, end to end, against a real zone.

Owner Research is the right Sprint pick for four reasons. It has the cleanest five-day input and output contract. It replaces a real cost line you are already paying. You named it as the lowest hanging fruit on April 14. And it teaches the reusable agent-building primitives (structured input, web lookup, validation, structured output) that every later agent in the chain inherits.

Each day ends with something that runs.

Day 01
Frame & Golden Example

You hand me one real zone with its fourteen properties. We define the input schema and what a "good" output record looks like. Quality target locked.

Day 02
Build the Lookup

Wire the agent: take an address, search, extract entity, owner, contact channels. First end-to-end run on three properties.

Day 03
Validation & Accuracy

Add the second pass: cross-check email validity, normalize phone, flag stale data. Run on the full fourteen, score against your existing records.

Day 04
Hand-off Pattern

Wire the trigger: target locked → fourteen records dropped into your CRM or sheet of choice in outreach-ready format. Brent walks the loop.

Day 05
You Operate It Live

You run the agent in front of me on a zone you have not seen. I watch. We tighten anything that surprises you. The agent goes into daily use Monday.

Day Six, you wake up, drop a target into the system, and walk to your morning coffee while it produces fourteen verified contact records. That moment is what makes the rest of the chain real.

06 / The Rest of the Boot Camp Month

Weeks Two through Four extend the agent into daily use and ship the second build.

Sprint Week proves Agent Number One. The remaining three weeks of the boot camp do two things in parallel: harden the first agent into a daily habit on your team, and build Agent Number Two against the highest-volume manual workflow you have already named.

Week Two
Days 6 to 12
Operate & Train. Owner Research goes into daily use across active zones. We pick one teammate (Brent or Victor are the obvious candidates) and train them to drive the agent without you in the room. Edge cases get logged and fed back into the agent's prompt and validation.
Weeks Three & Four
Days 13 to 30
Build Agent Two: Offer-Writing. The 80-offers-a-year pain you quantified on April 23 becomes the second build. You hand me a real signed letter of intent as the Golden Example, plus your current pricing-parameter logic per client. Output is a three-page non-binding LOI on your existing form, ready for your review and signature. By Day Thirty, both agents are running side by side.

07 / Phase Two and Phase Three

Months Two through Six. The chain extends, then the swarm goes live.

After the boot camp, the build cadence relaxes from a daily sprint to a more sustainable pace. The order respects technical dependency: each agent gets built when its inputs are reliable.

Phase Two
Months 2 & 3
Market Plan Generator (Agent 03), then Corridor Monitor (Agent 04). Market Plan Generator turns the vague client void map into the tiered plan your team produces today by hand. Once it is reliable, we light up Corridor Monitor: the always-on swarm starts watching every zone you have defined and feeding fresh prospects into the active-deal engine. This is the moment Layer Two goes live.
Phase Three
Months 4 to 6
Long-Cadence Outreach (Agent 05), then Void Analysis (Agent 06). Long-Cadence Outreach ends the one-night-stand pattern. Void Analysis turns on the proactive-pitch engine: your team walks into a retailer meeting with a packet that says "this is what you should do, here is why, and here are the sites we already have." This is the secret advantage realized.
Parallel Track
Phase 3 onward
Accelerated Development Services. The April 23 conversation flagged ADS as a second-entity opportunity. Once the VRG agents are stable in Phase Two, ADS opens as a parallel track on the same platform, reusing as much of the chain as fits.
